Introduction to Computer

Definition of Computer

A computer is an electronic device that accepts raw data as input, processes it according to a set of instructions (programs), stores the data, and produces meaningful information as output.

The primary purpose of a computer is to convert raw data into useful information that can be used for decision-making and problem-solving.

Data: Raw facts and figures.
Information: Processed and meaningful data.

Basic Functions of Computer (IPO Cycle)

  • Input: The process of entering data into the computer using devices such as keyboard, mouse, scanner, etc.
  • Processing: The CPU (Central Processing Unit) processes the input data using instructions provided by programs.
  • Output: The result of processing is displayed using output devices such as monitor, printer, speaker, etc.
  • Storage: The computer stores data and information for future use in devices such as hard disk, SSD, or cloud storage.

Characteristics of Computer

  • Speed: A computer can perform millions or billions of calculations per second. This makes it extremely fast compared to humans.
  • Accuracy: A computer produces accurate results if the input and instructions are correct. Errors occur only due to incorrect data or programming.
  • Diligence: A computer does not get tired or bored. It can work continuously for long hours without losing performance.
  • Storage: A computer can store a large amount of data for a long period of time and retrieve it when needed.
  • Automation: Once instructions are given, a computer performs tasks automatically without human intervention.
  • Versatility: A computer can perform a wide variety of tasks in different fields such as education, business, healthcare, and entertainment.

Applications of Computer

  • Education: Used for e-learning, online classes, research, and examinations.
  • Banking: Used in ATM services, online banking, transaction processing, and account management.
  • Healthcare: Used to maintain patient records, perform diagnosis, generate reports, and medical imaging.
  • Business: Used for accounting, billing, payroll, inventory management, and data analysis.
  • Communication: Used for email, internet browsing, video conferencing, and social media.
  • Entertainment: Used for playing games, watching movies, listening to music, and graphic designing.
